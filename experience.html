Software Engineer Intern at Draper Laboratory, Summer 2014

I developed flight software and re-wrote a key software tool used to project optimal landing geometries for the Dream Chaser, a reusable space-plane designed to replace NASAs Space Shuttle to transport crew and cargo to the International Space Station. The tool, originally written in the 1980s, was clunky, out of date and riddled with cryptic patches. Although some of the math (i.e., linear algebra and multivariable calculus) and physics (i.e., matrix manipulation) was beyond my coursework, I taught myself enough to transform the archaic, slow, inefficient and incomprehensible code into a sleek, functionally decomposed, fully commented program. After rigorous testing, my program now is fundamental to analyzing Dream Chaser landing profiles based on initial and desired final conditions.
